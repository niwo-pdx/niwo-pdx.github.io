About Me

Our experiences inform every aspect of our lives, and for me I find this often manifests in unexpected and exciting ways. I am a computer scientist, but also a visual artist, a writer, a horticulturalist, a bilingualist, and ultimately a curious mind; I enjoy taking my varied interests and life experiences and finding creative approaches to the problems presented before me.

When I'm not studying, I'm usually pursuing various artistic endeavors, or getting outside on a walk or hike. When space permits, I love to garden. When it does not, I find myself deep down research rabbit holes on subjects like soil science.

Education

I’m an undergraduate student at Portland State University studying computer science, with an expected graduation date of March 2027. My areas of study include broader CS theory, but I've recently started to focus more on databases, security, and web development.

I also have a certificate in International Business with Honors from Ohara Business and Public Service College in Kitakyushu, Japan.

Employment

I have years of experience working a number of customer facing positions - from service industry to help desk technician. I am comfortable in both taking a leadership role, and with stepping back to support others as the situation demands. I have seen, more often than I'd like, how not to do things. These lessons (while challenging) are ones I consider to be nearly as valuable as learning the correct approach.

Because of this, I've gone out of my way to try and build genuine connections and community with the people I engage with - both academically and professionally. I try and surround myself with those who love to teach and to exchange ideas, and as a result I've been able to foster rewarding relationships with people for the sake of mentoring and being mentored.

Research

I’ve recently joined the Database and Internet Privacy lab (DIPr) at Portland State University, and am participating in an undergraduate research program through CRA. At DIPr, I’m working with Ph.D. student Anadi Shakya (alongside Ambika Vyas) on her project addressing efficient implementation of fine-grained access control in database management systems.
